About Me

I am currently a research associate at the LiGHT Lab (Meditron) at EPFL / Yale University. My work spans reinforcement learning, synthetic data generation and large language models. In 2025 I led the reinforcement learning/ reasoning team for the Meditron project and spearheaded post-training and distillation efforts that resulted in significant gains on general and medical benchmarks. I also co-designed our model evaluation pipeline and optimized training at scale using vLLM, DeepSpeed and Axolotl.

Prior to this role, I contributed to the development of Meditree, a tree-of-thought inference method for medical diagnosis, and built synthetic data pipelines for structured medical guidelines. During my MSc at EPFL I also worked part time as a software engineer at Irbis Consulting where I developed an Electron app to automate bidding documents and created a deep-learning captcha solver in PyTorch.

I hold a Master's degree in Data Science from EPFL (GPA 5.4/6) and a Bachelor's degree in Communication Systems.
