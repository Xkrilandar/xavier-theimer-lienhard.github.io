About Me

I am a Machine Learning Research Engineer with the LiGHT Lab (Meditron) at EPFL. I work end-to-end across training, evaluation, and deployment for open medical LLMs. I began in 2023 during the early Meditron finetuning sprints, later leading sub-projects in Meditron-Reasoning (RLHF + reasoning) and Meditron-Synthetic (safe clinical data generation).

My focus is on efficiency, interpretability, and auditability: I co-designed evaluation pipelines, led RL and reasoning post-training on HPC, and built Meditree, a multi-agent inference method mirroring clinical reasoning. Recently, I co-led Apertus-Meditron-FO, a fully open adaptation of the Swiss Apertus models for medicine, including an open medical corpus and finetuning of 8B/70B variants.

I hold a Master’s degree in Data Science from EPFL (GPA 5.4/6) and a Bachelor’s degree in Communication Systems. Outside of work: running, climbing, Wikipedia rabbit holes, and a probably unhealthy enthusiasm for coffee and brunch.

Current Projects

Auto‑Moove

Direct medical feedback optimization using Moove evaluations. Building reward models and GRPO pipelines that turn clinician Likert ratings and model comparisons into stable learning signals. Deliverables include reward-model reports, reproducible training scripts, and benchmarked improvements over Meditron baselines.

  • Reward modeling with preference and scalar signals
  • GRPO training with evaluation gating
  • Reproducible Axolotl/DeepSpeed training recipes

LiGHT‑SeqDx‑Bench

A sequential diagnosis benchmark and harness focused on clinically meaningful multi-step reasoning. Includes baselines, metrics beyond accuracy, and a public leaderboard submission.

  • Eval harness with error taxonomy
  • Baseline suite and leaderboard-ready outputs
  • Analysis of diagnostic failure modes
